Assessment
This skill is internally consistent for a payment/checkout automation service: it legitimately needs a CREDITCLAW_API_KEY and the runtime steps (register, poll or webhook, get one‑time decryption keys, decrypt card data locally, fill merchant forms) are what you'd expect. However, it gives any agent with access to the key the ability to spend funds and handle raw card data. Before installing: 1) Verify the provider (creditclaw.com) and prefer a published homepage/repository — the registry metadata here shows no homepage/source, which lowers trust. 2) Treat CREDITCLAW_API_KEY like a high-value secret: store in your platform's secrets manager and do not paste it into other services. 3) Require owner approval_mode (default 'ask_for_everything') or stricter spending guards until you fully trust agent behavior. 4) If you provide a callback_url/webhook endpoint, ensure that endpoint is controlled by you and is secured (HTTPS, verify webhook signatures, store webhook_secret safely). 5) Audit transactions and enable alerts; limit agent autonomy (don't allow automatic spending) unless you intentionally trust the skill and account settings. If you cannot verify the provider or are uncomfortable with automated spending, do not install or only enable in a heavily restricted/test environment.
